About Craig

Two Decades of Winning
at Every Level

Craig Furstenau has been on the ice since age 3 in Michigan. He competed through juniors and finished his playing career as captain of the Oakland University Grizzlies — then immediately started building one of the most decorated coaching careers in the state.

As head coach at Oakland University (2002–2005), Craig posted an .800+ win percentage, won back-to-back GMHL titles, captured the 2004 national championship, and reached the 2005 national final. He was inducted into the Oakland University Athletic Hall of Fame in 2005.

With Little Caesars AAA, Craig won back-to-back state titles and the 2008 national championship. His Belle Tire AAA squad finished #1 nationally in 2014–2015. Players Craig has developed have gone on to play in the USHL, NAHL, and college hockey — and the game's best have sought him out for their own development.

The Craig Furstenau Method

What Sets This Apart

Every elite coach has a system. Craig's is built on four interlocking pillars refined over 20+ years and every level of the game.

01
01

Foundation Skating

Edge work, crossovers, transitions, and first-step explosiveness aren't just drills — they're the base every other skill is built on. Craig breaks skating down to its mechanical roots and rebuilds it correctly, at any age.

  • Edge control & balance
  • Acceleration mechanics
  • Backward & transition skating
02
02

Puck Mastery

High-repetition puck handling, shooting, and passing under pressure. Craig trains hands to work automatically — so when the game speeds up, the skills don't slow down.

  • Hands-to-feet coordination
  • Shooting mechanics & release
  • Puck protection & possession
03
03

Hockey IQ & Compete

The mental side of the game separates good players from great ones. Craig coaches players to read the ice, anticipate plays, and compete at a level that goes beyond physical skill.

  • Positional awareness
  • Decision-making under pressure
  • Compete level & battle habits
04
04

Athlete Wellness

Craig's background in sports nutrition sets him apart from most coaches. Hydration, fueling, recovery — he teaches players what to do off the ice to perform better on it. Most coaches skip this. Craig doesn't.

  • Sports nutrition fundamentals
  • Hydration & performance
  • Recovery habits & longevity
