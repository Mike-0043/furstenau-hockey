About Craig

Two Decades of Winning at Every Level

Craig Furstenau started playing organized hockey at age 3 in southeast Michigan, competed through juniors, and finished his playing career as captain of the Oakland University Grizzlies.

As a coach, Craig built one of the most decorated résumés in Michigan hockey. At Oakland University (2002–2005) he posted an .800+ win percentage, won 2 GMHL titles, captured the 2004 national championship, reached the 2005 national final, and was inducted into the OU Athletic Hall of Fame. With Little Caesars AAA he won back-to-back state titles (2007–2008) and the 2008 national championship. His Belle Tire AAA team finished #1 nationally in 2014–2015.

Craig teaches hockey fundamentals to all ages with a focus on foundational skating and puck control — and the nutrition and wellness knowledge to become a complete athlete.
